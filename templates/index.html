Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about phishing and how to protect yourself

What is phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime in which targets are contacted by email, telephone, or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data such as personally identifiable information, banking and credit card details, and passwords.

Why should I care about phishing?

Successful phishing attacks can cause financial loss, put your personal information at risk, and compromise your data and systems. The purpose is to collect sensitive information to gain unauthorized access to protected data and networks.

Types of Phishing Attacks

Deceptive Phishing: Fraudsters impersonate legitimate companies using threats and urgency.

Spear Phishing: Targeted attacks on specific individuals using researched personal information.

Other techniques: Email phishing, smishing (SMS), vishing (voice calls), whaling (executives), and malvertising.

How to protect yourself?

  • Use strong passwords and avoid sharing personal details on social media
  • Verify suspicious emails by contacting the organization directly
  • Only open attachments from trusted sources
  • Check for typos and grammar errors in communications
  • Never provide personal information via email or unsolicited calls
  • Keep your software and antivirus updated
